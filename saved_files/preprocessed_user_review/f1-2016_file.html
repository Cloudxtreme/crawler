    Combining driver aids with opponent difficulty gives a wide range of options that should open this game up to all gamers. From an arcade game with no damage and unlimited rewinds to full length races more akin to a simulator, you can set things up to suit you.

    The complexities of F1 are all present, but done in a way that is fairly intuitive and easy to understand. Strategy becomes much more important than in previous versions, and as a result it adds layers of enjoyment to racing. The monotony of grinding out laps is broken by the decision making you need to have the right strategy to maximize your finish.

    Presentation is one of the major successes in this game for me. The feeling of F1 is all around you, and it enhances the experience immensely. Graphics look very good on my system, although I have had some FPS problems which I hope will be solved with better optimization down the road.

    VR support is the only thing missing from this title in my opinion.

    Overall, great game. A lot of fun to play and great to look at. The depth of career mode is going to keep me hooked for a while. I was skeptical about this release after years of being disappointed, but this version is the best one yet.    … Expand
