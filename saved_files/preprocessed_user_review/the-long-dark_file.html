    With an emphasis on atmosphere and exploration, Hinterland have beautifully crafted a brilliant game, although at the moment still in beta, and with just a sandbox mode (Nov 2015).

    It is essentially a game of balance. You need to gather resources, and be able to defend yourself at all times, from wolves, bears, the extreme cold, starvation, dehydration, fatigue, dysentery, infection and falls.

    It taps into the innermost fears most have, fear of the dark, the unknown, vulnerability, loneliness and, of course, death. It does so in the most innocuous ways, leaving you always thinking 2 steps ahead/behind, and always looking over your shoulder. But make no mistake, unless you get mauled by a bear while resting in your sleeping bag, this isn't a jump-scare fest. The scares are slow burning, realistic and ever-present.

    In sandbox mode, there are currently limited areas to explore, but any new game takes quite a while to succumb to the inevitable, long dark. My record life span is 177 (in-game) days ... but I am working on that. I normally put aside two weeks to play, a few hours each evening.

    What I love most about this game is the crisply defined atmosphere. The weather sound effects in particular are amazing, whether indoors or outdoors, it always feels real. The soundscape makes up for the lack of variety in visible fauna, as they are always there... in the background.

    There are some downsides and bugs, which will undoubtedly be fixed/improved in time, but the game, even in beta is incredibly stable, or fixed very quickly by the developers when problems are raised.

    This review will need to be deleted when the full game is released, because it will probably be a completely different experience than it is at present, with a story, characters and an end game. It could well be the best game of 2016, and will no doubt win huge acclaim and awards.

    For now, this is one of the best games I have played in a long time. In an age where gamers are ever increasingly taken for mugs, (day 1 DLC anyone?) this is a refreshing change, and a hugely entertaining experience.    … Expand

There's four in this game: temperature, hunger, thirst and energy. Make fire to keep yourself warm in a raging blizzard, scavenge for plant and animal sustenance, boil water over aforementioned fire and find shelter to rest. All the while you scavenge for supplies, whether it is by shooting deer, fishing or looting cabins in the quiet apocalypse. Beware wolves that are attracted by the smell of blood and the sounds of a cold survivor struggling to find his next shelter.
