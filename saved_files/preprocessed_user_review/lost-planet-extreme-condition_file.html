Lost Planet is a fair shooter even for the PC. Fast paced action, meaty and solid weapons as well as properly sized bosses (a rarity nowadays, but I would expect no less from the people behind Mega Man). The story is almost hilariously bad, but I suppose that is to be expected. At least it provides an excuse for showing us the well endowed women in the cut scenes. The different game modes aren't exactly numerous, but there's enough variety to keep it interesting. Also, I find the additional freedom offered by the characters wire hook to be a welcome addition, as it adds vertical movement and bullet dodging to the normal shooter controls. Thankfully, the stages have also been well crafted and created with this in mind. My last positive note is that the game is beautiful, it's definitely up there with the best. The physics may not be very impressive (rocks rolling downhill seem like they were made of papier mache or something), but it's definitely a looker. This game might be worth a purchase for all the visual goodness alone. My only big gripe with the game is regarding the Vital Suit controls. Vital Suits are small mechs, armored walker machines that usually pack heavy firepower. They are equipped with thrusters to enable jumping and limited flight, and may sometimes transform into secondary modes, such as hoverbikes. The translation to PC has not gone painlessly for these beasts, as it is sometimes quite hard to direct their firepower when, for example, trying to strafe incoming fire. This is mostly because of the placement of the buttons that enable special mech functions, such as having to press X+sideways to initiate a thruster powered gliding maneuvre. Predicting the lenght of jumps may also be quite hard. Capcom should have consulted games like Mario 64. Add a big shadow under the mech, shift the camera up a bit so you have a good view of the landing area and presto, your jumping would have improved greatly. To conclude, the game has some flaws, inculding a short-ish single player campaign, and weird controls (which can, however, be reconfigured), but is generally a very solid title, and definitely has a japanese feel that is often hard to find in PC titles.
