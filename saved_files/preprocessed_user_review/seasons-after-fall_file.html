As    Seasons After Fall
    A beautiful game with too much repeating.
    Season afterfall is a metroidvania styled 2d puzzle platformer where you play a seed that takes control of a fox..
    you use this fox to platform to the different season guardians for their power,
    once you reach them, you use your newly acquired powers to get back and go for the next…
    Your powers are changing the seasons..
    As you change the season the items in the environment change, plants will die or come to life..
    you can even stack effects like adding water to a guizer to freeze it when its taller for example…
    The first half of this 6 hour game is great, the world is beautiful,
    there a nice voice telling you the story of the guardians, the seasons, and reminding you where oyu should be going if you get lost,,
    the puzzle platforming is fun, and fluent…
    running through these levels as a fox feels freeing…
    And then the game just goes on repeat..
    you have to go back to these sections 2 more times to get to the end game..
    sure seeing different sections to these levels.. but this is where the puzzles start piling on and the game starts feeling like a chore..
    ruining the initial freeing feeling this game gives..
    Not to say the game gets bad..
    it just gets more stressful and its no fun to go through locations you’ve already been to even if you have new powers this time or things are a little different…
    It just made me miss the first few hours of the game that much more
    Seasons After Fall starts out great and had potential to be one of my favorites of the year,
    but the 2nd half drags it down big time and feels almost like a completely different game
    It takes a great game and makes it just almost great…
    The game would’ve greatly benefitted to completely different paths for the 2nd half rather than retracing a lot of your steps and essentially activating switches
    I give Seasons After Fall
