    There is good reason why there are no negative reviews. This game pretty much had no flaws. Imagine that, flawless multiplayer FPS? With rather decent solo play vs bots (even slightly challenging on Godlike).

    If game had problems, it was players, to be precise cheaters, which caused creation of UTPure and other anti-cheat "add-ons", only problem was that it caused a bit of lag/delay    There is good reason why there are no negative reviews. This game pretty much had no flaws. Imagine that, flawless multiplayer FPS? With rather decent solo play vs bots (even slightly challenging on Godlike).

    If game had problems, it was players, to be precise cheaters, which caused creation of UTPure and other anti-cheat "add-ons", only problem was that it caused a bit of lag/delay when playing. But that was one and only problem.

    Frankly I never cared about how good this game looks (it did look good, well Unreal engine, kinda guaranteed good look). What was more important was movement in game. Along with Quake 3, this game had probably best and most natural movement I ever experienced in FPS. Plus ability to dodge (well most of us used it to move around via dodge pretty much constantly) was rather unusual that day. Since then, no game had such perfect in game movement (also it was something to master).

    Game itself never tried to hamper you with artificial problems. Today, most games have artificial difficulty created by need to fight game mechanics and opponents in same time. Its stupid and mostly it has negative impact on any competitive gaming. UT99 had nothing like this. You had only one limit, which was YOU. I think apart Q3 Arena, very few games are same category. Today probably none.

    Back in day, it was bit hard on computer hardware (as UT engine wasnt exactly undemanding). Few years later, everything could go over 100 FPS. Which was good.

    Netcode wasnt perfect (well not from today perspective), but it wasnt that hard to learn to compensate for your ping (there were mods like zero ping, which worked quite fine). Still if you take for example today BF4, it had amazing netcode. :D

    It had rather nice sound (still has), supported EAX, surround if Im correct. As far as graphics goes, it was possible to play via D3D or OpenGL (what can do that today?).

    Game itself was reasonably customizable, bindings, HUD colors/size, crosshairs (actually most today games CANT do that, tragic really). Via console you could enable even some more advanced settings.

    Gameplay wise, it was balanced around player skill. Every gun was usable, and if we dont count Redemeer, nothing was overpowered. Sure some weapons (Bio rifle for example) were far from easy to use, and Flak cannon was "noob" gun of sorts, but if you achieved decent skill in game (and your opponents too) it was rather clear that there is no such thing as "noob" gun and everything is usable.

    Another thing that was nice (apart headshots and ASMD combo) is for example basic gun, Enforcer. Which is simple pistol. You could pick another one and shoot both, you could use either primary attack (accurate, slower) or secondary attack (faster, less accurate). Thing is, even basic enforcer did 25 dmg (you had 100 when you spawned, boostable to 199). Which means you could kill most ppl with 4 shots. Show me today FPS, where you can kill someone with 4 shots from basic gun (obviously with dual Enforcers it was much easier).

    Then there was ability to combo. Mostly known was ASMD rifle, secondary attack sent big blob of blue energy towards target, if you hit that blob with primary attack it went caboom and mostly killed everything in radius. Advanced players were able to do this while moving.

    Rocket launcher despite being rather slow compared to Quake counterpart (you could still rocket jump tho, it just hurt much more) could do primary attack, secondary attack and then loaded primary (hold and load up to 6 rockets) or loaded secondary and on top of that if you loaded primary and then hit secondary as you released, you could alter attack to send rockets in tight cluster (was pretty much instakill if you hit with it). If you count it, you have 6 different ways to attack with single weapon.. impressive, huh?

    Beauty of multiplayer was that you could literally kill with everything as long as you were good. Game was pretty unforgiving for beginners, today mainstream pub would run back to mama crying after one Deck 17 game. Cause this game wasnt made for ppl which are unwilling to improve themselves, there were no easy ways to kill. You didnt get award for participation in game, you didnt get actually any award apart good feeling when you saw your nickname at top of end game stats. Thats exactly why I loved and still love that game.

    This game is pure essence of what is (or should be) competitive multiplayer game. Clean, fast, easy to play and hard to master. Everyone is equal and you play to beat others, not game mechanics.

    Since then, there wasnt any FPS game I would love so much. Also probably none so good. Sure I spent years on CoD4, later on BFBC2 (which would be probably second on my list), but nothing is close to UT99. And unfortunately I doubt there ever will be something at least as good as UT99, cause gaming industry as whole is degenerating and pretty fast..    … Expand
