The story remains unchanged from the original,and is a bit short in length.

    The interface is simple though sometimes a bit cluttered with many interaction possibilities crammed into a single area. The spacebar provides a "reveal" function which identifies each interactable area, alleviating this problem.

    The audio is pretty good, some of the games soundtrack comes through a bit too loud but the quality is good. The background audio in game (Jazz musicians, Tribal Drummers, etc..) is great and really helps set the mood during certain sequences.

    Graphically there is something to be desired. The graphics themselves aren't bad per se, but the game tries to blend 3D characters against 2D backdrops without depth scaling. This leads to 3D characters getting inexplicably taller as they move towards objects intended to be farther away, such as doors. The players character enters one door at normal height yet barely fits under another door in the distance. Aside from the poor blend, the visuals are good.

    I will be very clear and objective about this remake of Gabriel Knight: Sins of the Fathers, so all who read this review can understand exactly what I mean with my words.

    1) In regards of graphics, this remake is amazing, with scenarios full of details, colors and clear passion in each one. Although, 3D models are not as good as I was expecting. In fact, I think that 2D and 3D does not combine well.

    2) In regards of musical scores, this remake is also great, in the same line of the original, with new instruments and, again, a lot of passion.

    3) Puzzles and extras are also good and I don't have anything to point out.

    4) But in this remake of GK there is one particular thing that does not surpasses the original and that thing is the interface. This remake's interface is somehow strange, sometimes unresponsive and annoying, especially when we try walking to a certain area of screen and extra options show up undesired.

    5) Animations contribute to increase the atmosphere, but GK's animations react badly. Each time messages are shown on screen, skipping them makes ALL game animations run fast-forward until they reach the start loop point, which is awful, annoying and, most importantly, unacceptable for a new game like this. The original version did not suffer from this and even in 1994 this "strange thing" was also not an acceptable thing!

    In SUMMARY, Gabriel Knight is a decent game, with a great story, excellent graphics but with serious failures that should already be solved before launch date. I understand the passion, dedication and efforts around this project, but if the original did not miss in its aim, this one cannot definitely fail! For all this, and for the great annoyance certain things in the game causes to players, I'd give it just a 6 out of 10. I give it a 6 because I'm a GK admirer and lover, otherwise I'd rate it 5 out of 10.
