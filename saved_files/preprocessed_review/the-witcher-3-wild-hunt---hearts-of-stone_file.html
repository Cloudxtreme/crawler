There's not much to be said about gameplay - the expansion introduces no new concepts, so if you liked it before, you'll like it now, and if you didn't like it before, well, it's still the same.

    The principal new addition to the game, mechanics-wise, comes in the form of an alternate way of upgrading your gear, the Runewright. Essentially, instead of adding glyphs and signs to your gear, you can add a single "Runeword", which has a very powerful effect, but takes up all three slots (it won't work with gear with less than three available slots). The whole process of unlocking the various levels of Runewords was expensive, time-consuming, and, in my opinion, not worth the effort - I'd rather just make due with my usual hodge-podge of glyphs and signs. All in all, the Runewright doesn't really fit anywhere - if you've already beaten the game, odds are you don't need what he's selling, and it you're starting a new game, his services are too expensive for you to realistically afford.

    My one complaint about this expansion (and it is a minor one) is that it feels too disconnected from the main story.
