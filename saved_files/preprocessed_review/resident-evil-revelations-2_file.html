The big exception to this rule Resident Evil: Revelations 2. It is a horror game, but not in the normal alone-in-the-dark kind of way. The game has you guide two couples through the story. Which is a bit less scary. This could disappoint horror fans, as isolation is a cornerstone in any horror story, but it allows squeamish people like me to play it. Perhaps one can say that it isn't a real horror game?

    Revelations 2 strongest part is probably the team system. It should allow the team members to be played by different players in a sequel, but in this one one is controlled by the player and the other by the computer. You can switch between the two at will and you have to, as it will help overcome obstacles. You can also give the other member commands. Each member has certain strengths that you can use to the teams advantage.

    The first couple is Claire and Moira. Claire is the older of the two and the fighter. She gets to handle the weapons, such as the pistol and shotgun. Moira is a teen and has some special abilities. She can blind adversaries with her torchlight and has a crowbar that she can use for bashing attacks and followup attacks.

    The other couple consists of Barry and Natalia. Barry takes the role Claire has and is the fighter. Natalia, a child of around twelve has special abilities in that she can sense danger, spot treasure and see invisible enemies. She can't fight so Barry has to do all the fighting, but she can tell him where to shoot.

    The trick is to have the members of a couple work together. Especially Natalia's ability to see invisible enemies will be important.

    Each couple finds itself on the same forlorn island that used to be operated by the Soviets until it was abandoned to the local populace. After a while a group of people representing a company showed up to ostensibly operate the mine. But the inhabitants started to get suspicious when people started to disappear or change. The island itself eventually became dangerous as evil creatures start to roam around.

    Claire and Moira are the first on the island because they are kidnapped and locked up in a prison. They manage to escape and try to flee the island but find that the only way to get away is to find the one who is in charge. The path to freedom leads through the heart of danger.

    Barry arrives six months later to find Moira, his daughter after she has send out an emergency broadcast. When he sets foot on the island he encounters Natalia. Not knowing what to do with her, he takes her along, and he soon finds out that she can help him with her special abilities.

    The nice part in the game is the interaction between the two members of a couple. Especially that between Barry, as a replacement father, and Natalia. Claire and Moira act more like friends. Time is spend on making this connection believable. Another is the one that links Barry with Moira.

    The progress over the island is less impressive. There are various dangers, felt more keenly because of the lack of ammunition, especially by the first couple. There some traps, puzzles and boss encounters, nothing really surprising when you think of it, but coupled with the team system it works nicely. The narration isn't top notch for a book or a movie, but for a game it is good.. An additional factor that makes this installment better is that the movie has a different atmosphere as it's direct predecessors. It more resembles the earlier creepy resident evil installments than the action oriented later ones.

    The lowdown: Resident Evil Revelations 2 isn't a very remarkable game, but with it's team work mechanism, creepy mood and more story based angle allows for a much needed revitalization of the Resident Evil series. It shows a possible way to go, but it depends Capcom to take that route. Will it improve on this or will it take another road entirely? It remains to be seen.    … Expand
