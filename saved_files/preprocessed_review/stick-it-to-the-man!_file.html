    +Puzzles are fun and bizarre. If you like Monkey Island puzzles, you'll feel right at home here.
    +Art and visuals are really well-done. They do feel slightly reminiscent of Psychonauts, but the cardboard cutout look gives them a style all their own, and at times looks frighteningly realistic.
    +Music is catchy and effervescent, played by live musicians. Sounds very similar to Grim Fandango.
    +There are some very funny, well-timed jokes, which is a hard thing to do in games.
    +Platforming physics are pretty solid and predictable.
    +Enjoyable world to play in.

    Iffy:
    -It can be hard to select whose mind you want to be listening to at times.
    -"Stealth" parts with henchmen are OK for a while, but get tiresome, and in the last few levels, feel like they detract from the flow of the story and puzzles.
    -Sometimes the voice actors sound like they're ad libbing and the dialog comes out a bit too "bro speak."

    -It's pretty much impossible to describe this game without referring to a Tim Schafer game. But I mean that in a good way.    … Expand
