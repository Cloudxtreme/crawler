    In unravel you play as yarny, I’m not sure what yarny is but he’s made out of yarn, and that’s all that matter because this is important
    the entire game is using this yarn to overcome every obstacle that comes your way.
    You can use your yarn to swing across gaps, you can use it to create bridges, pull items, and to tie items together,
    These few simple principles are what make up the entirety of yarnys journey
    Unravel is not a difficult game, but it is challenging
    I never had to look up a solution to a puzzle, but it did stump me numerous times, making me feel like a genius when I finally figure out the simple solution that’s been staring me in the face for 5 minutes
    The puzzle aspect of this game is great, but I feel like there is just a little too much emphasis on it
    The levels in Unravel are gorgeous and so full of life, really putting you into the perspective of this character..
    You never feel like you’re in a generic themed level…
    Each level feels like this is a place from someone’s memory
    And they are, as you complete each level, a scrap book if filled in to give more insight into the story going on..
    A story just as beautiful as the levels
    You could stop almost anywhere during the game and be amazed at what’s going on in the breathing world around you
    Only problem is you have to actively do this, Unravel is almost constantly testing your brain,
    There are a few action sequences sprinkled in, but far less than there should be..
    It would be nice if there were more slow down moments in the game…
    And by slow down I mean for your brain, not the gameplay
    A little more emphasis on some linear platforming here and there rather than just puzzle platforming stacked on top of each other
    While I say this though, I never felt overwhelmed while playing unravel which is common for me when I play this type of game
    I actually couldn’t put the controller down..which is even more of a surprise considering each level took me anywhere from 20 to 50 minutes to play through organically…
    I just feel like so much work, care and love went into crafting these levels, and there’s never an opportunity to truly enjoy them…. To stop and soak them in...
    All of your focus and brain power is going right into the task at hand that the backdrop can sometimes become a blur…
