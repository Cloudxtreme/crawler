It's a formula that in my honest opinion works much better than the one used in Unity, where they removed some of the best features of the previous game. The naval combat and exploration is awesome, there are some epic battles waiting for you, whether you try to conquer a fort or instead choose to engage on a legendary battle with a ship, much like in ACIV:BF. I love the sense of freedom, and all the activities that are at my disposal and that can be re-done almost whenever I like, such as "harpooning", engage on battle with enemy ships to gather resources or reinforce my fleet to the naval campaign, or if I'm just tired of going around on the sea, I can also go hunt or try to plunder a couple of warehouses! All of this has been removed from Unity, and it was nice to have them back on this game. So yeah, all in all it's being an extremely positive experience, the game doesn't look as big as ACU for completion's sake, but it feels very fresh all the time. I'm still on a very initial stage of the game, but I'm positive this feeling of freshness will remain until the end. To be honest, I think this is the second best game of the series, in parallel with ACIV, only surpassed by ACII, which introduced incredible innovations at the time.

    Now, more technically speaking, of course the textures aren't as crisp as in Unity, which was predictable, given the system requirements and the fact that this is basically a port of a game released for the previous generation consoles. Still, they do the job, and not being as detailed and crisp as Unity, it's certainly more beautiful, with its jungles, caves, settlements and last but not least, almost endless sea :)

    The gameplay is also on par with what we've been used to on the series, the parkour is not perfect, so the land exploration and hunting can be annoying a couple of times, when you jump down the tree instead of climbing up, or when you miss by inches a rocky ledge and fall to your death. The gameplay really shines on the naval battles, sometimes I feel that this is the main purpose of the game, since it's so very well thought and designed.

    I don't know what else I should say about this game, so maybe I'll just mention a couple of cons just as to my review doesn't sound too biased. As I said before, the parkour isn't perfect, resulting in a couple of accidents when you try to do some basic looking manoeuvres when you're on foot, and the enemies' AI is also poor, I think it's never been easier to hide from enemies after you've been detected. Sometimes I'm hidden in a bush, an enemy comes to me, I kill him and hide him instantly in the bush, and there was an enemy a couple of meters away looking on my direction and he doesn't even notice his mate has been assassinated.

    All in all, I think it's a great game, I'd easily recommend it to anyone who's new to the series or to anyone who's already a veteran. Of course that if you played ACIV:BF and didn't like it, you'll probably won't enjoy this one either. However, if you liked ACIV:BF, you'll definitely love Rogue, so go ahead and get it. The series are probably taking a turn to a totally different direction, I'm not very optimistic about it after playing Unity, so let's enjoy this "old" take on the series while we can.

    The Good:

    -Interesting combat. (I know this is in the bad too, let me explain) In Assassin's Creed Rogue you actually spend most of your time fighting against Assassin's, and they like to employ different tactics than your average AC enemies, these enemies will let you think you escaped by slowly "losing" you, and just when you think you're safe you get air assassinated.     -Naval combat. Although there is a little less here than there was in AC4, the massive areas you can explore on land make up for this, you can still raid and plunder unsuspecting ships, then steal their gold just like the gold old days, and another option they added (Which is very unappreciated!) is the ability to exchange the ship you boarded for gold, if say, you have full health and you don't want it to be a part of Shay's fleet. Overall, I think the naval combat was better in Assassin's Creed 4: Black Flag, this game comes very close and is still just as engaging. Also enemies can now board your ship, exciting!
    -PC Port. Now I know that everyone has had some bad experiences with previous Ubisoft ports, so you may be a little cautious as to whether or not you should pick this game up, so I came to assure everyone that this is a PC port that looks great and doesn't introduce any new bugs (at least none that I have found).
    -What I thought was very lacking in AC Unity is very, very present in AC Rogue, gadgets. You have so many gadgets and gear that it keeps gameplay very interesting all the time. That's just one weapon, we still see the return of the classic rope dart and throwing knives and a few others such as the gas mask, which comes quite in handy when fighting an army of Assassin's.

There are buildings (forts count too) all over the world that you can renovate (for stone and money) and then start collecting income. Once you start to spread your influence all over North America you really start bringing in some dough. What this does is it makes it so money is not a constant grind, but something that you can earn while doing other things that actually interest you. Crafting with pelts and upgrading with materials plundered while out at sea is also very fun and time consuming. It is always rewarding when you finally get that final upgrade.

    Conclusion:
    If you are in to the Assassin's creed (or naval/pirate) genre and you have a PC then I think this game is a must have. The game plays great, is optimized, and rarely to never crashes. It has an engaging story from the point of view of the classic enemies, fun as hell naval combat, and a time consuming material/economy/crafting system that will keep you raiding and plundering for hours. I gave this game a 9 because it is not perfect, and has a couple of bugs and can seem repetitive after playing for an extremely long amount of time. I was not a big fan of Black Flag (mostly because of the story, but also because some of the "side content" was mandatory to progress, which had me lose interest eventually), but did like ACIII.

    I didn't think I'd enjoy Rogue much, as most people said it was very similar to BF, and it is. However, this has become one of my fave AC-games.

    Story
    This is where Rogue gets a lot of points. The game manages in a very nice fashion to build a bridge between ACIII and Unity. This game is a prequel to AC III, and it was quite fascinating to play and see how the Templars got so powerful, how Achilles ended up the way he did before you meet him in AC III etc.

    Of course, you know the deal: This is about an assassin becoming a Templar. There are some parallels to the whole Anakin Skywalker-thing - brash student with lots of potential goes to the dark side and destroys his former organisation.
    However, Shay still has mercy left. He works with the Templars, but has good intentions and actually still does good things. At the end, there is a direct segue to Unity...or back to the beginning, if you want to (re)play AC III.
    Great stuff!!

    Gameplay
    This is very similar to BF. The parcouring and combat is totally similar to ACIII and BF, but there is a very strong focus on naval battles. There are some small improvements, and some differences (icebergs, for example), but mostly, its like BF... capture forts, destroy or capture ships, salvage them or add them to your fleet.
    Fortunately, no diving this time, which I HATED in BF. Instead, here you examine and explore shipwrecks, wghich is where you find blueprints to improve your ship to the maximum level.

    Characters
    I liked all the main characters. Haytham, Achilles, and several other chars from the series are involved. Shay is a cool guy.

    Misc stuff
    Different outfits (if you completed other AC games, you can wear Altairs and Ezios robes), whaling, a new weapons (air rifle, which replaces the blowhorn from BF, plus a grenade launcher, which you can use to toss berserk, sleep or shrapnel grenades)

    Verdict
    The game sometimes is forgotten because it came out so close to Unity, and Unity created a lot of press (a lot of it negative), but: It's an important entry to the series. While AC1-Revelations were pretty straight forward, the next part of the series started almost from the end, and Rogue leads back to AC III and closes the cycle... and leads to Unity.
    It is like a mix between BF and ACIII, but where it really shines is with the story.
    I finished the game (main story, quite a lot of side content) in 16 hrs, but there is more I could have done... find all blueprints and upgrade my ship to go for legendary ships, unlock all buildings and settlements etc.
