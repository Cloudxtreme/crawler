    A big attraction for me was the ability to be able to use, create or modify cars and    I have been with AC on the PC since it first became available, having previously been a big Forza and Gran Turismo player until all my peripherals became obsolete on the next-gen consoles. The problem with AC is that it becomes a different game with every upgrade - sometimes better, but usually worse.

    A big attraction for me was the ability to be able to use, create or modify cars and tracks made by normal users, although it does involve quite a learning curve. It gives the opportunity to be able to drive some of the more unusual or classic vehicles on equally unusual or classic tracks. Some of these 'Mods' are very good, but many are downright awful. The situation is further complicated by the fact that after most of the game's many and frequent upgrades the 'Mods' need to be updated to allow for new tyre models or new physics or, in particular, revamping of the engine sounds - which will have disappeared. Understandably most 'modders' get totally cheesed off with this and simply abandon the whole affair.

    The handling was pretty good from the off. At about version 1.3 it was excellent. We are now, at the time of writing, on version 1.10 and I'm afraid to say that it has gone right down the toilet by now. There seems to be some weird universal tendency for racing games (or sims) to go for the 'boomerang effect', whereby the back-end steps out, either deliberately or through over-enthusiasm and no matter how much you try to correct with less throttle and opposite lock, it just suddenly swings violently back the other way. This can either throw you right off the track facing the wrong direction or you will continue fish-tailing down the road.

    Front-wheel-drive cars also seem to have too much grip even when you reduce the road surface traction and are very difficult, if not impossible, to chuck about. Which is another great shame.

    You really need a wheel and pedals for this game too. The graphics are very good, although the latest version has just mucked up the screen resolutions so anything above full HD isn't fullscreen anymore. Bear in mind that some user-created tracks and cars are never going to look good no matter how brilliant the game gets.

    Gameplay as such is pretty mundane. Tune-ability and paint/livery changes require intensive evening classes as you have to doctor the code.

    All-in-all Kunos have shot themselves in the foot as far as making it a game for the 'modding community', which must be feeling quite betrayed by now.
