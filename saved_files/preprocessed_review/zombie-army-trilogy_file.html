ZAT has the soul of a cooperative game, unlike Call of Duty's zombies spinoff,I have no experience with the main Sniper Elite series, but I felt inspired to get Zombie Army Trilogy for myself and my friends some time ago after watching Mr. Sark's amusing playthrough of it. While shooting nazis, zombies and even nazi zombies is nothing new, I think ZAT represents a refinement of such. ZAT has the soul of a cooperative game, unlike Call of Duty's zombies spinoff, which still tries to shoehorn competitive elements in to the gameplay.

    As a spinoff of a game about sniping, ZAT has the unique quality of having you use a sniper rifle as your primary weapon, with a choice of secondarys from among various submachine guns, shotguns and the panzerfaust, and a choice of pistols for backup. I recommend the Webley. Depending on the difficulity you're playing on, you must take into account things like bullet drop, wind speed and your character's heartrate when shooting, and giving you prone stance as well as crouching. You can also adjust the density of the zombies.

    This standalone release of Zombie Army includes the first two episodes that were add-ons to Sniper Elite 2, as well as a new third episode with new enemies, more interesting level design and better writing, and is overall a superior experience, though the first two are hardly bad, just very straightforward.

    Overall, ZAT is not a flashy or unique game, but it offers solid four-player coop third-person shooting, lots of explosives and multi-kill opportunities, and the ability to play as the cast of the Left 4 Dead games if that floats your boat. And, well, you get to shoot zombie Hitler in the face. I'd say that's worth the asking price.    … Expand
