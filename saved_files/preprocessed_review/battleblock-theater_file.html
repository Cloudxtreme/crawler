    CONS: - Slippery platforming means you don't always go where you want – Some segments are really frustrating – Game doesn’t feel like it goes anywhere

    REVIEW: So I watched the intro and thought to myself: “hey that narrator sounds really funny, that might be a fun game!” I was both right and wrong: the narrator is funny until you’ve heard everything he has to say many times, and the game is fun in some modes. There are some really interesting puzzles which actually quite stretch your brain, too. It isn’t until the end where timing has to be precise, which is a good thing, because your character feels like a slow, earthbound sucker (double jump notwithstanding). This means that you can breeze through most of the game relatively quickly—if it holds your interest for long enough, anyway. I know that I got bored and didn’t come back for a few months. Still, the multiplayer modes seem fun enough when there are actually people playing them (I got the fireball weapon, and it was way too much fun burning the poor other players).

    There are significant drawbacks, too. This game doesn’t really compare favorably to any AAA platformer I’ve ever owned in terms of mechanics. I don’t know, maybe you are supposed to get a charge out of being slow and hopping tiny jumps, but anybody can do that in real life… Speaking of, there are some intensely frustrating moments after the difficulty ramps up and timing suddenly becomes critical. There are generally enough checkpoints to make this feel less annoying, but that won’t keep you from running out of time on the last finale levels. Also, aside from the difficulty going up, the game doesn’t feel like it is going anywhere. Sure the cutscenes are funny, but they lead you to believe that there will be some kind of coherent ending—which turns out not to be the case.
