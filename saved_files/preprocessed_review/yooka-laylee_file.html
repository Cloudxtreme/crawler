    Let's start off with the gameplay

    The gameplay is smooth, simplistic, and fun! Not really anything new compared to B-K / B-T except for Tonics. Tonics are specialities that you can unlock and choose to have one active at a time - though it would have been better to have at least up to 3 considering there's 16 of them!
    You also buy moves in this game, learning them along the way in each world, and they can be used in previous worlds to accomplish something you couldn't before. So there are is a reason for backtracking here.
    You also have arcade games which you also unlock in every new world which feature minigames that you can play and get a high score in. If you want to, of course.
    And now we come to the collectibles, which are perfect. There aren't too few nor too many, it feels like the perfect amount when you first enter the world and when you expand the world, and all of them feel meaningful. You also don't collect for just the collecting part because you are unlocking something.

    ---
    Let's move on to the levels

    There are "only" five levels, yes, but you can EXPAND them to become even BIGGER. When you play through it the first time it will feel like a B-K-sized level, and when you expand them they will feel more like a B-T-sized level. So you do have a reason to replay all 5 levels if you want to, or not.
    However, I do agree that 5 levels can be a bit too few, and I would love at least 7-8. Let's hope the DLC for this game will have newer levels and/or story.
    Furthermore, the level design may not be as good as in B-K / B-T, but they are definitely aesthetically pleasing - and not as much fun to traverse - than its spiritual prequels. Though it is way better than DK64. I would definitely say that World 4 is the so-called dark horse in this game and many might agree with me.

    ---
    Let's end off with the story

    It's a 3D platformer, the story isn't that important. You have a villain you have to defeat, an evil corporate bee who seeks all of the world's literature, along with his assistant Dr. Quack. You enter his domain, Hivory Towers, collect the Pagies to unlock Grand Tomes (Worlds) all the way until you reach his HQ.

    ---
    In retrospect

    Like I said; if you've played B-K / B-T this is a game for you, especially if you miss 3D platformers. If you don't, then this isn't for you.
